History

Bagauda fishery School

The School of Fisheries Studies at Bagauda, established in 1971 by Audu Bako, the first military governor of Kano State, was conceived as a cornerstone for fish hatching and fish food production in Northern Nigeria. During the early 1970s, under Bako's visionary leadership, the school became a center of excellence for aquaculture, providing essential training in fish farming techniques and production methods. Its establishment was part of Audu Bako's broader agricultural reforms, aimed at boosting food production and rural economic development, crucial to sustaining Kano's growing population.

The school's operations flourished for several years, offering specialized courses in aquaculture, fish hatchery management, and fish feed formulation. This positioned Kano as a significant player in Nigeria's fishing industry, with graduates from the school playing key roles in the growth of fish farming across the region.

However, due to various administrative and economic challenges, the school was forced to close its doors during the years that followed. This setback significantly hampered the state's plans to become a hub for fisheries and fish production.

A major turning point came during the administration of Rabiu Musa Kwankwaso, who served as governor of Kano State from 1999 to 2003, and again from 2011 to 2015. Kwankwaso, known for his passion for education and infrastructure development, undertook the revival of the School of Fisheries Studies as part of his broader agenda to rehabilitate key educational institutions across the state. This revitalization breathed new life into the school, with modern facilities and updated curricula that catered to contemporary challenges in fish farming. The revival was celebrated by the local community and agricultural sector, as it offered new opportunities for employment, food production, and economic growth.

Unfortunately, after Kwankwaso's tenure ended, the school once again fell into disrepair and was closed down. This period of inactivity caused many to lose hope that the institution could recover.

However, in a new wave of optimism, Abba Kabir Yusuf, the current governor of Kano State, has made it a priority to restore the School of Fisheries Studies to its rightful place. With the strong leadership of Alhaji Kabir Yusuf, the new director of the school, the revival efforts have gained momentum. Alhaji Kabir Yusuf had a personal connection to the school, having contributed his own resources to sustain it during its shutdown. His dedication to the school's mission has been instrumental in its current revival.
