Introduction

As a premier institution for aquatic studies, the Bagauda School of Fisheries Studies is a diverse learning environment that brings together individuals from various backgrounds. It is a place where students acquire knowledge and skills while respecting each other's cultural, racial, and religious identities. Graduating from this institution signifies your development into a knowledgeable and responsible citizen.

Campus Life

Campus life at Bagauda School of Fisheries Studies offers numerous opportunities but also presents challenges. Students, especially those in their teens and twenties, may encounter:

  • Peer pressure
  • Temptations related to substance abuse
  • Involvement in inappropriate activities

Navigating these challenges requires maturity and a commitment to positive behavior.

Conduct Expectations

Students are expected to demonstrate responsible conduct and adhere to school regulations. When a student fails to maintain proper behavior, their peers and faculty are encouraged to provide guidance. However, serious breaches of conduct may lead to referral to the Student Disciplinary Committee, which includes both staff and student representatives.

Dress Code

In line with institutional policies, students are required to adhere to a specific dress code to promote professionalism and respect within the school community. The following guidelines apply:

Male Students

  • Collared shirt and trousers
  • Complete native attire or two-piece native dress
  • Decent jeans or formal wear

Female Students

  • Knee-length skirts or long trousers
  • Long or short sleeve blouses
  • Kaftan or Boubou
  • Complete native dress or formal attire

Prohibited Dress

The following attire is not permitted:

  • Body-hugging clothing, spaghetti tops, and tubes
  • Transparent apparel or clothing that exposes sensitive body parts
  • Inappropriate headwear (e.g., face caps, panama hats)

Misconduct

Misconduct includes any behavior that violates school regulations, covering various aspects of campus life, such as:

  • Examination conduct
  • Regulations governing dormitory occupancy
  • Traffic rules on campus
  • Student registration protocols
  • Club and association activities
  • Dress code adherence

Responsibility

Each student is individually responsible for their actions and is expected to encourage their peers to comply with school regulations.

Offenses and Sanctions

The following are examples of offenses and corresponding sanctions:

General Offenses

  • Illegal rallies or assemblies: Expulsion
  • Disturbances or conflicts between student groups: Warning or Expulsion
  • Religious intolerance: Expulsion
  • Possession or distribution of illegal materials: Expulsion
  • Fighting fellow students: Warning, Rustication, or Expulsion
  • Willful damage to school property: Replacement cost or warning/expulsion

Serious Offenses

  • Theft: Expulsion
  • Physical assault: Warning or Expulsion
  • False claims or forgery: Expulsion
  • Rape: Expulsion
  • Substance abuse: Expulsion and referral to appropriate authorities
  • Possession of weapons: Expulsion and referral to authorities

Additional Misconduct

  • Unauthorized use of school premises: Warning or Expulsion
  • Harboring individuals without permission: Expulsion
  • Failure to return school property: Withholding of academic results and certificates
