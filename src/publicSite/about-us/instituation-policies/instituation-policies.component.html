• Governance and Administration

Policy Statement: The institution is governed by a Board of Trustees responsible for overseeing administration and ensuring adherence to the institution's mission and vision.

Roles and Responsibilities: Define the roles of the Board, the management team, and academic committees.

  • Academic Affairs

Curriculum Development: All programs must align with industry standards and be reviewed annually for relevance.

Assessment and Evaluation: Regular assessments will be conducted to ensure that academic programs meet learning outcomes.

Academic Integrity: Plagiarism, cheating, and other forms of academic dishonesty will not be tolerated and will be subject to disciplinary action.

  • Admissions and Enrollment

Admissions Policy: Criteria for admission will be based on academic qualifications and relevant experience in fisheries studies.

Enrollment Procedures: Detailed steps for application submission, selection criteria, and enrollment confirmation.

Retention Strategies: Support systems will be in place to assist students in their academic journey, including academic advising and counseling.

  • Student Life

Code of Conduct: Students are expected to adhere to standards of conduct that promote respect and integrity within the academic community.

Disciplinary Procedures: Clear procedures for addressing violations of the code of conduct, including potential sanctions and appeals.

Student Organizations: Guidelines for the formation and operation of student organizations, ensuring alignment with the institution's mission.

  • Financial Management

Tuition and Fees: Transparent policies regarding tuition fees, payment plans, and financial aid options.

Budgeting: Annual budgeting processes will be implemented to ensure financial stability and accountability.

Financial Aid: Eligibility criteria and application procedures for scholarships, grants, and loans.

  • Human Resources

Employment Practices: Policies regarding recruitment, hiring, and retention of faculty and staff based on merit and qualifications.

Professional Development: Opportunities for continuous professional development and training for faculty and staff.

Workplace Conduct: Expectations for professional behavior and procedures for addressing grievances.

  • Health and Safety

Emergency Procedures: Comprehensive emergency response plans for various scenarios, including natural disasters and health crises.

Health Services: Access to health services for students and staff, including mental health resources.

Risk Management: Policies to assess and mitigate risks associated with campus activities.

  • Diversity and Inclusion

Equal Opportunity: Commitment to promoting diversity and inclusion within the institution, ensuring equal access to education and employment.

Anti-Discrimination Policy: Prohibiting discrimination based on race, gender, religion, disability, or other protected characteristics.

  • Technology and Data Management

Acceptable Use Policy: Guidelines for the appropriate use of technology resources and internet access.

Data Protection: Policies to safeguard personal and sensitive information of students and staff in compliance with relevant laws.
